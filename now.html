What I'm Doing Now

Inspired by Derek Sivers' nownownow.com
Last updated: Monday, June 10, 2024
  • Reading: I'm currently reading The Hard Way by Lee Child - one of the Jack Reacher books. I haven't read a Reacher book in a little while, and I forgot how exciting they are. I've also been reading The Invisible Life of Addie LaRue by V. E. Schwab, which is one of the most beautiful books I've ever read. I actually started Addie LaRue last summer, but I overdid it with my courseload last fall and got completely burned out (and had zero free time anyway) so I'm slowly getting to a point where I can actually finish the book.

  • Washington, D.C.: I've been absolutely loving walking around DC and exploring the city. The Sculpture Garden is my favorite place right now, although I've been enjoying Dupont Circle recently as well. Normally I just like to go and read a book, drink some coffee, and maybe get some lunch while I'm there. I also went to the Pride Parade in DC this past Saturday, which was a lot of fun (thank you DMV weather for not raining).

  • Photography: I've been taking a lot of pictures on my phone recently. UMD's campus is absolutely beautiful when the trees have leaves on them, and DC has so much beautiful architecture - it sort of feels wrong not to take pictures all the time. I've been posting a few on social media, however I'm currently trying to figure out a reliable way to be able to display them here without making the site's GitHub repo too big or anything. I've been playing around with nginx on an AWS Lightsail instance, but there's still some configuration stuff that I need to work out first before I can use it to host my photos.

  • Music: I've been listening to a lot of David Bowie recently. I mean a lot. I've also been listening to a lot of The Last Dinner Party and Chappell Roan this year. Some specific songs (not by any of those artists) that I've been obsessed with recently are "Close to You" by Gracie Abrams and "Moon Girl" by Ha Vay. There's also the odd mix of classical trumpet solos/excerpts, and A Love Supreme by John Coltrane has been one of my favorite albums to listen to over the past month or so. Fun fact: I've been naming some of my papers for school after various songs/albums - check out my Writing Samples and see if you can find them!

  • Academics: I'm finishing up my undergrad at UMD College Park and will be graduating in December with a B.S. in Computer Science, B.A. in History, and Minor in Trumpet Performance! I'm looking forward to being done, but I'm also not particularly looking forward to working for the next 40-or-however-many-years :/

  • Academics, cont.: On that note, I have been developing an increasing interest in programming language theory. I took a compilers class last fall and a runtime verification class this past spring and absolutely loved both of them. I'm probably doing an independent study this upcoming fall on property-based testing, however that hasn't been finalized yet. Perhaps this will give me an excuse to not fully launch my career yet and instead go to grad school for a bit? I guess we'll just have to see.

  • Misc.: I've been thinking about starting a blog for a while now. I love breakfast sandwiches, so one thought I had was to review breakfast sandwiches in the DMV. I'm also going to start playing trumpet and riding my bike again - I haven't done either since last summer for various reasons, but trumpet is always fun and there's some great bike trails not too far away that I used to go on a lot. Now I just have to try not to crash my bike again.

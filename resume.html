Selected Papers

  • "Running Away from the Future"(History Intern at Enslaved.org, December 2023)
    • This research paper examines runaway indentured servants and enslaved people in seventeenth-century Virginia and Maryland, along with their surrounding legal landscape, to establish their specific reasons for running away and the considerations they had to make. By accounting for their conditions and increasingly harsh punishments, including with respect to race, this paper argues that the changing legal landscape served as both a motivation and a deterrent for those running away.
  • "Welcome to the West, it's been waiting for you"(Civil War and Industrialization at UMD, November 2023)
    • Through analyzing Laura Ingalls Wilder's Little House series and Willa Cather's My Ántonia, this paper argues that, in the decades following the westward expansion seen in the mid-to-late nineteenth century, popular media romanticized it to serve as a distraction from the pressing events of World War I and the Great Depression. This paper shows that these books simultaneously gave contemporary readers a means of escape and painted a nuanced picture of life in the American West.

Selected Papers

  • "Women, War, and Higher Education"(Women and War in U.S. History at UMD, May 2024)
    • Through examining women's experiences at the University of Maryland from roughly 1922-1960, this paper argues that World War II and the surrounding war effort on the home front were paramount to the increasing and lasting acceptance of women's involvement on college campuses. Firstly, the paper explores the general historiography of the field, encompassing sources from historians, economists, sociologists, and other researchers. The paper then examines original research, which was conducted at the University of Maryland's Special Collections, which contains records regarding UMD's and the State of Maryland's history. By looking at records of Adele Stamp and other notable figures from UMD's history, the paper uses correspondence, scrapbooks, and newspaper articles to makes its argument nuanced and effective.
  • "The Tortured Gatsbys Department”(Emergence of Modern America at UMD, April 2024)
    • By treating Sinclair Lewis' Babbitt, Nella Larsen's Passing, and F. Scott Fitzgerald's The Great Gatsby as primary sources, this paper (alternatively titled “The Rise and Fall of Jay Gatsby and the Spiders from Mars”) argues that the Roaring 'Twenties were in many ways a reflection of the Gilded Age. The paper looks at specific examples in each book of anti-union sentiments, government and business corruption, racism, decadence, and consumption of culture to show these similarities. Through incorporating a small number of lighthearted sentences, the paper effectively keeps the reader interested while making a strong argument.
